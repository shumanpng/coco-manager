## Claim / Question / Decision

With multi-project workflows, re-onboarding to a project after days/weeks away costs ~1 hour per switch. With limited 1.5–2h work blocks, that consumes most of the available time. The context manager's `/catchup` + worklog/state/planning trail collapses this to minutes — this is the system's single biggest productivity lever, validated by the author's own use.

## What would change my mind

A user trial shows `/catchup` does not meaningfully shorten re-onboarding (e.g., still takes >30 min on average), or the cost of maintaining the trail (worklog discipline, distillation overhead) exceeds the time saved.

## Next step

Use this framing in user-facing copy (tutorial intro, README, launch).

## Notes

Caveat: value prop weakens for very small projects finishable in a few days — there's no context-switching tax to pay. Pitch line: "You spend an hour re-onboarding yourself every time you switch projects. What if that took 5 minutes?"
